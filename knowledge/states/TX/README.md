# Texas Security Guard Licensing

This directory contains regulatory documentation for Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS) Private Security Bureau licensing requirements.

## Regulatory Authority

- **Agency**: Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS)
- **Division**: Private Security Bureau
- **Website**: https://www.dps.texas.gov/section/private-security

## License Types

| Type | Initial Training | Renewal Training | Renewal Period | Min Age |
|------|------------------|------------------|----------------|---------|
| Level II Non-Commissioned (Unarmed) | 4-6 hours | None | 2 years | 18 |
| Level III Commissioned (Armed) | 45 hours | 6 hours | 2 years | 21 |
| Personal Protection Officer | 15 hours + Level III | 6 hours | 2 years | 21 |

## Important Changes (2024)

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Effective January 1, 2024**: New Level III applicants must complete:
> - Minimum 10 hours of in-person firearms and defensive tactics training
> - MMPI psychological examination

## Level II Non-Commissioned (Unarmed)

### Requirements
- 18 years of age or older
- 4-6 hours Level II training course
- Covers fundamental responsibilities and legal requirements
- DPS background check

## Level III Commissioned (Armed)

### Initial Requirements
- 21 years of age or older
- 45 hours of approved training including:
  - Classroom instruction with written exam
  - Firearms course of fire for proficiency
  - Handcuffing, holds and restraints
  - Weapon retention
  - Range qualification
- 10 hours in-person firearms/defensive tactics (as of 2024)
- MMPI psychological exam (as of 2024)
- DPS/FBI background check

### Renewal Requirements
- 6 hours continuing education
- Firearms re-qualification

## Personal Protection Officer (PPO)

- Must hold Level III commission
- 15 hours additional PPO training
- 6 hours continuing education for renewal
